Why use a Monero paper wallet?

Using a Monero paper wallet is the most secure way to store XMR because it keeps your private keys completely offline. Unlike online or exchange wallets, a paper wallet cannot be hacked remotely.

Paper wallets are ideal for long-term cold storage, cryptocurrency gifting, or maintaining privacy while holding Monero securely. Whether you are new to crypto or an experienced user, generating a Monero paper wallet ensures total control over your funds.

Security recommendations
  • Always generate your Monero paper wallet offline on a clean, trusted computer.
  • Write down your 25-word mnemonic seed and store it in a safe, dry place.
  • Do not take screenshots or save your seed on internet-connected devices.
  • Consider making two copies of your seed stored in separate secure locations.
  • If possible, use a metal backup to protect against fire or water damage.
How to import your Monero paper wallet using monero-wallet-cli

You can easily restore your Monero paper wallet using the official command-line wallet (monero-wallet-cli). Follow these steps:

  1. Open a terminal on your computer that has the Monero CLI tools installed.
  2. Run the deterministic restore command: 
    monero-wallet-cli --restore-deterministic-wallet
  3. When prompted:
    • Wallet file name: choose a new wallet name (e.g., coldwallet).
    • Mnemonic seed: enter your exact 25 words.
    • Restore height: enter a block height or date near your first transaction to speed up scanning.
    • Password: set a strong local password.
  4. You can connect to a remote node if you don’t run your own: 
    monero-wallet-cli --daemon-address node.moneroworld.com:18089 --restore-deterministic-wallet
  5. After restoring, verify your public address and balance using: 
    balance

Once verified, your Monero funds are accessible. Remember that anyone with your mnemonic seed can spend your XMR — keep it offline and private.
